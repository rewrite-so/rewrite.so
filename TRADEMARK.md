✅ **Self-host the code for personal or internal team use** — including for
your company's internal tools — without permission, as long as the
deployment is not made publicly available as a paid or branded service.

✅ **Fork the code and distribute modified versions**, provided you choose a

✅ **Refer to rewrite.so in articles, tutorials, comparisons, reviews, and

**hosted service, SaaS offering, browser extension, or paid product** that
you operate.

❌ Use the rewrite.so logo, color scheme, or visual identity in your fork or
derivative product.

❌ Imply endorsement, partnership, or affiliation with rewrite.so when none
exists.

project's [Acceptable Use Policy](https://rewrite.so/aup).

## Naming forks

If you fork rewrite.so and distribute it, please:

1. Pick a name that is clearly your own (e.g., `MyEditorAI`, `AcmeRewrite`).
2. Replace all visible occurrences of "rewrite.so" in the UI with your new
   name before distributing or hosting.
3. Optionally credit the original project: *"Based on rewrite.so
   (https://rewrite.so), used under the Apache 2.0 License."*

This protects both you (avoids accidental trademark infringement) and us
(prevents user confusion and brand dilution).

## Why this policy exists

Open source is a gift to the community. Trademarks are how a project
preserves the user trust attached to its name — so that when someone
recommends rewrite.so to a friend, the friend knows what they're getting.

This policy is modeled on the trademark policies of established open-source
projects such as Mozilla, WordPress, and Rust.
